Logic Jumps are what makes a form powerful. It allow you to create complex structures within the form. In this way, consumers only see the questions relevant to them, which saves a lot of time. It's like guiding your consumers through the form.
The Feedback Analytics Logic Jumps are advanced. This opens the possibility for some extra things, such as cross selling and customer segmentation.
A Logic Jump is comparable with the branch of a tree. The three is a question, and could have multiple branches. Depending on the choice someone makes on question A, this person will either go to question B or C. If you have a Multiple Choice question, with 4 different answers, it's possible to give each answer another branch.
Different question branches can meet each other again at another question. To visualise this you could think of two rivers meeting and fusing together, forming a single channel.
But this isn't necessary, it's also possible to give each route a different ending. Read this about end screens
To add Logic Jumps to a question, you should already know how to create a form with Feedback Analytics. If you dont have this knowledge yet, reading up could help.
It's also possible to look back at more than one answer filled in by the consumer, which makes logic jumps extremely powerful. When a person fills in a series of questions the system can look back at all those answers and select the corresponding route to take in the form.
The Logic Jumps by Feedback Analytics make use of boolean logic, which is a mathematical language, really close to the english language.
